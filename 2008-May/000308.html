Yes, I know this is the official way now.
But I think it's not enough. We need to get some privileges.
We maintain localization for tens or hundreds of packages and the
localization itself can be updated quite often (every time a single
message gets added or edited in original package). Doing it like this
can be a very lengthy and bureaucratic procedure.
My experience with gtkdialog has taught me that we need to have a
privilege to release SRPM update to contrib server WITHOUT waiting for
permission from packager, our duty should only be to inform packager
about such release in advance (that privilege should be of course
strictly limited to changes concerning i18n or l10n).
Without such privilege, we will never catch up with mainstream and our
contributors will get frustrated because their work will take a lot of
time to get into the repos.
